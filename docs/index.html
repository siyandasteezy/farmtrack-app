Section 2
Farm Plan
Configure your farm profile, GPS boundary, and named zones

Farm Plan — the central configuration page for your farm setup.

Farm Profile
Enter your farm name, address, country, total area, and precise GPS coordinates (latitude & longitude).
Farm Boundary
Draw a circular or custom polygon geofence on a satellite map. Used by Animal Tracking to detect escaped animals.
Zones & Locations
Define named areas like Paddock A, Barn 1, Field B. Zones populate the location dropdowns in Livestock and Sensors.
Boundary Shapes
Choose between a simple Circle (centre + radius) or a Polygon (click to draw any shape) for your geofence.
1
Click "Edit profile"
Fill in farm name, country, area, and GPS coordinates. Save to apply.
2
Click "Edit Boundary"
Choose Circle or Polygon mode. For Circle: drag the pin or click the map to position centre, then adjust the radius slider. For Polygon: click the map to add vertices (minimum 3), then Save.
3
Add Zones
Click "+ Add zone", give it a name (e.g. "Paddock A"), select a type and colour. These will appear as suggestions in all location fields across the app.
💡
Tip: If you set your farm's latitude & longitude in the Farm Profile and the boundary is still at the default location, saving your profile will automatically move the boundary centre to your real coordinates.

Section 3
Livestock
Manage all your animals across 11 species

The Livestock page — species tabs, filters, search, and a full animal table with inline actions.

Auto-generated Tags
Ear tags are automatically generated per species (e.g. CT-001 for Cattle, SH-001 for Sheep). You can override them with your own tag.
11 Species Supported
Cattle, Sheep, Goat, Pig, Horse, Poultry, Rabbit, Alpaca, Duck, Deer, Bee — each with breed-specific options.
GPS Tracker Assignment
Attach a registered device or enter a custom ID. Optionally add GPS coordinates for immediate map visibility. Device ID is not required if you provide coordinates.
Filter & Search
Filter by species, status, or location. Full-text search across tag, name, and breed.
1
Add an animal
Click "+ Add animal". Select species — the tag auto-generates. Fill in breed, date of birth, weight, sex, location, and status.
2
Assign a GPS tracker
Click the 📡 icon in the Actions column. Choose a registered device or enter a custom device ID. Optionally enter GPS coordinates to place the animal on the map immediately.
3
Edit or remove
Use the pencil icon to edit, the trash icon to delete. The eye icon shows a full read-only view of the animal record.
💡
Location suggestions: Once you define Zones in Farm Plan, they appear as suggestions in the Location field when adding or editing an animal.

Section 4
Health & Vet
Medical records, vaccinations, and scheduled vet care

Health & Vet — track treatments, vaccinations, and upcoming scheduled visits.

Health Records
Log vaccinations, treatments, check-ups, surgeries, and more against any animal by tag.
Upcoming Vaccinations
Switch to the "Upcoming Vaccinations" tab to see all scheduled vaccination due dates.
Vet Cost Tracking
Each record can include a vet cost. Total spend is summarised in the header stats.
Edit & Delete
All records support full edit and delete. Errors are shown inline at the bottom of the form.
1
Click "+ Add record"
Select the animal tag, health event type (Vaccination, Treatment, Check-up, etc.), date, and vet details.
2
Track ongoing events
Set the status to "Ongoing" for treatments in progress. These appear in the Ongoing count on the summary bar.
3
Schedule future vaccinations
Add a record with a future date and status "Scheduled". It will appear in the Upcoming Vaccinations tab.

Section 5
Farm Sensors
Real-time IoT monitoring across your farm

Sensors — live dashboard of all IoT sensors, manual reading entry, and device registration.

Add Sensors
Register any sensor type — temperature, humidity, air quality, water level, weight scales, and more. Assign a location from your defined zones.
Manual Readings
If a sensor goes offline, enter a manual reading with a reason. Manual readings are flagged with an (M) indicator.
Device Setup
Register hardware devices via the Device Setup tab. Supports MQTT, HTTP REST, and WebSocket protocols with auto-generated configuration code snippets.
Alerts
Sensors outside their min/max thresholds trigger Critical or Warning alerts shown at the top of the dashboard.
💡
Location suggestions: Sensor locations are populated from your Farm Plan zones. Define your zones first, then sensor locations auto-suggest from those names.

Section 6
Feed & Nutrition
Track feed types, stock levels, and per-head consumption

Feed & Nutrition — manage feed types, restock inventory, and monitor consumption per species.

Feed Types
Create custom feed types per species with daily per-head consumption rates, units, stock levels, and cost per kg.
Restock
Log feed orders per feed type to replenish stock. Stock levels update immediately.
Edit & Delete
Each feed type can be edited or removed. All changes are persisted automatically.
Empty State
When no feed types exist, a guided empty state with a clear "+ Add feed type" prompt is shown.

Section 7
Equipment & Tickets
Manage farm equipment and raise maintenance tickets

Equipment — track all machinery and raise maintenance tickets when something needs attention.

Equipment Register
Add equipment with name, type, serial number, location, purchase date, and status (Active / Maintenance / Retired).

Categories
Animal Welfare, Health & Biosecurity, Environmental, Traceability & Record-Keeping, Organic & Certification, and more.

Section 10
Animal Tracking
Live GPS map with geofence alerts for escaped animals

Animal Tracking — live satellite map showing all GPS-tracked animals against your farm boundary.

Live GPS Map
Satellite and street map views. Each tracked animal appears as a coloured species marker. Click any marker to open the detail panel.
Escape Alerts
Animals outside the farm boundary are flagged in red with an "OUTSIDE" badge. An alert banner shows their distance from centre and last-seen time.
Polygon & Circle Geofence
Supports both circular (radius-based) and custom polygon boundaries defined in Farm Plan. Point-in-polygon detection for complex shapes.
List View
Switch to List view for a full table of all animals with battery level, distance from centre, last-seen time, and device ID.
1
Set your farm boundary
Go to Farm Plan → Edit Boundary. Choose Circle or Polygon and position it over your farm on the satellite map.
2
Assign trackers to animals
In Livestock, click the 📡 icon on any animal row. Enter the device ID or GPS coordinates. Save — the animal will appear on the map immediately.
3
Monitor from the map
Open Animal Tracking. Animals within boundary show green; those outside show red. Click a marker to see health history, battery level, and exact coordinates.
⚠️
Battery alerts: Animals whose trackers have less than 20% battery appear in the low battery warning banner. Replace or charge tracker units promptly to maintain continuous monitoring.
